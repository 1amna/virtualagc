Introduction

Like the AGC's software, the AGC's hardware (electrical and mechanical design) evolved over time.  In other words, not all AGC electrical schematics are created equal ... you have to know which specific AGC model number you're interested in, since its schematics may be different than other AGC models.  On this page, we attempt to break down the AGC/DSKY hardware design on a unit-by-unit basis, and provide the mechanical drawings and electrical schematics for those specific AGC/DSKY units.

There are additional (sometimes overlapping) electrical or mechanical drawings available which don't (or haven't yet) been integrated into this particular framework.  Such information can be seen in the "AGC electrical schematics" section of our document-library page.

Except where otherwise stated below, all drawings for p/n 2003993-x were contributed by Eldon Hall to klabs.org, and all drawings for other AGC/DSKY part numbers come from the so-called AGC Handbook, contributed to Virtual AGC by Don Eyles, the raw scans for which can be seen on our Internet Archive site here:  TBD.

Additional information for used for cross-referencing the various drawings to specific AGC or DSKY part numbers and/or serial numbers came from a couple of sources:

  • Section 3.7 of the AC Electronics Final Report on Contract NAS9-497 and tables 7-IV and 7-VI of the Instrumentation Labs Final Report on Contracts NAS9-153 and NAS9-4065 can be used to relate many AGC and DSKY serial numbers to the specific missions (or in some cases, other purposes) for which they were used.  In some cases, there is a discrepancy between these two documents (e.g., which DSKYs were assigned to LM-10), in which case the latter document was chosen to be authoritative.
  • Table 8-II in AC Electronics LM PGNCS Manual ND-1021042 (volume 2) and Table 3-IE in volume 1 can be used to obtain drawing numbers of electrical schematics corresponding to the different AGC and DSKY assembly part numbers.

Explanation of AGC/DSKY Part Numbers

The way different part numbers of AGCs or DSKYs are related to one another is that you start with a given assembly with a certain part number (such as Block II AGC with p/n 2003100-011), then you apply an Engineering Change Proposal, or ECP for short, such as ECP 322 (described as "computer wiring changes"), with the result that you now have a different assembly p/n 2003100-021. 

The list below shows how all known Block II AGC part numbers are interrelated according to the principle just mentioned.  Many of these changes clearly are not electrical in nature, or else refer to intermediate or test versions of the AGC, so to simplify it a little bit I've highlighted in red the path (I think) from the earliest Block II AGC to the AGC p/n(s) used in flown missions.  (Sometimes there are multiple paths to get to the same part number, if the ECPs are applied in different orders.  For example, p/n 2003993-061 + ECP 815 = p/n 2003993-071 + ECP 719 = p/n 2003993-111.  I've only bothered to show one such path below in the few cases where that happens.)
  • p/n 2003100-011
    • p/n 2003100-021 — p/n 2003100-011 + ECP 322 ("computer wiring changes")
      • p/n 2003100-081 — p/n 2003100-021 + ECP 474 ("manufacture test connector jumpers to ground certain gate inputs")
      • p/n 2003100-031, -041, -051 — p/n 2003100-021 + ECP 301 ("thermal instrumentation")
        • p/n 2003100-061 — p/n 2003100-031 + ECP 474 ("manufacture test connector jumpers to ground certain gate inputs")
        • p/n 2003100-071 — p/n 2003100-041 + ECP 474 ("manufacture test connector jumpers to ground certain gate inputs")
        • p/n 2003100-081 — p/n 2003100-051 + ECP 474 ("manufacture test connector jumpers to ground certain gate inputs")
      • p/n 2003200-011 — p/n 2003100-021 + ECP 176 ("computer module vibration") + ECP 226 ("aluminum to magnesium conversion of AGC trays") + ECP 254 ("computer multilayer board layout") + ECP 257 ("redesign of rope and erasable drivers") + ECP 258 ("redesign of power supply module") + ECP 259 ("redesign of erasable memory") + ECP 324 ("sense amplifier threshold voltage stability change") + ECP 351 ("alarm module temperature stabilization of warning integrator and improve oscillator fail alarm") + ECP 368 ("improved power supply module relays") + ECP 402 ("clear circuit driver modification") + ECP 403 ("strobe adjustment") + ECP 470 ("random workmanship vibration")
        • p/n 2003200-021 — p/n 2003200-011 + ECP 440 ("'clear rope' driver circuit modification") + ECP 443 ("replacement of short screws") + ECP 447 ("incorporation of plastic pads under tray A&B covers") + ECP 460 ("addition of jumper wires in tray A") + ECP 478 ("paint exposed surfaces on mid-tray spacer")
          • p/n 2003200-031 — p/n 2003100-021 + ECP 486 ("cut pins on AGC power supply to remove 28VDC regulator")
            • p/n 2003200-041 — p/n 2003200-031 + ECP 474 ("manufacture test connector jumpers to ground certain gate inputs")
              • p/n 2003200-051 — p/n 2003200-041 + ECP 518 ("standby change on computer") + ECP 564 ("implementation of flat pack specifications ND 1002359A & ND 1002358B") + ECP 604 ("incorporation of E-memory vibration pads")

            • p/n 2003993-011 — p/n 2003200-031 + ECP 452 ("wiring change to accommodate auxiliary memory unit") + ECP 474 ("manufacture test connector jumpers to ground certain gate inputs") + ECP 485 ("redesign power supply to remove 28VDC regulator") + ECP 501 ("implementation of flight processing spec") + ECP 505 ("implementation of flight processing spec ND-1002341 and new diode") + ECP 511 ("correction of computer noise SCAFAL problem")
              • p/n 2003993-021 — p/n 2003993-011 + ECP 518 ("standby change on computer") + ECP 631 ("replace RTV-102 with RTV-109")
                • p/n 2003993-031 — p/n 2003993-021 + ECP 564 ("implementation of flat pack specifications ND 1002359A & ND 1002358B") + ECP 604 ("incorporation of E-memory vibration pads")
